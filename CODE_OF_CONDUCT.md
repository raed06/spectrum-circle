# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

SpectrumCircle exists to support the autism and neurodiversity community.  
We are committed to making participation in this project a welcoming, respectful,
and safe experience for everyone — regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender
identity, level of experience, nationality, neurodivergence, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, empathetic,
and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- Using welcoming and **neurodiversity-affirming language**
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and lived experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Prioritizing the well-being of the community, especially its most vulnerable members
- Recognizing that contributors may have different communication styles — and that's okay

### ❌ Examples of unacceptable behavior

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission (doxxing)
- Using deficit-based or stigmatizing language about autism or neurodivergence
  (e.g., "suffers from autism", "high/low functioning" as a dismissive label)
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered harmful in a professional or
  community setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests,
discussions, code reviews, and any other communication channels associated with
SpectrumCircle. It also applies when an individual is representing the project
in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported by opening a private issue or contacting the project maintainer directly
via GitHub. All reports will be reviewed promptly and kept confidential.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.
They reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject any contributions, comments,
or issues that do not align with these standards, and to temporarily or permanently
ban any contributor for behaviors deemed harmful to the community.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

| Severity | Behavior | Consequence |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **1 - Correction** | Inappropriate language or minor unprofessional behavior | Private written warning + clarification |
| **2 - Warning** | A violation through a single incident or series of actions | Formal warning with consequences for continued behavior |
| **3 - Temporary Ban** | Serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior | Temporary ban from all project interaction |
| **4 - Permanent Ban** | Pattern of violations, harassment, or targeted harm | Permanent removal from the project |
